BetterInvest advances 70% of the contract value of broadcasting, OTT, and music rights to content producers via invoice discounting — then collects repayment once rights are monetised. Track record: ₹462+ Cr GTV, 102 contracts, 0% credit default rate. But when a film release date moves, OTT windows move with it — and investor repayments move too. Cascading delays had investor anxiety rising and fresh inflow stalled.

The Problem

  • Production delays — shoot extensions, release pushbacks, actor unavailability — cascading into repayment delays
  • HNI and retail investors receiving insufficient communication about why or when
  • The 0% default rate was a powerful fact — not being communicated actively enough
  • Investors conflating delay with default — a perception problem, not a financial one
  • Fresh investment had slowed as word-of-mouth sentiment softened
  • No structured retention or re-engagement strategy existed

What I Did

Reframed the Problem Internally First

This wasn't a repayment problem — it was a perception problem. The central insight: delayed repayment and default are not the same thing. Most investors were conflating the two. The campaign's job was to separate them clearly, repeatedly, and credibly.

Email Sequences — Transparent, Anchored on Track Record

Structured update emails explaining entertainment rights discounting in plain language. Each anchored on the 0% default track record and gave investors a clear picture of project-specific timelines. Tone: confident and honest, never defensive.

WhatsApp Outreach — Personal, Not Templated

Direct personalised outreach to HNI investors showing signs of disengagement. High-trust and conversational. This channel reactivated investors who had gone completely silent — a personal message from someone who understood their concern carried far more than any broadcast.

Investor Newsletters — Education as Retention

Regular newsletters that went beyond updates: how entertainment rights discounting works, why release delays are industry-structural not company failures, what a 0% default rate means in practice. Built understanding, not just reassurance.

BetterInvest had proven the model at scale — ₹462+ Cr GTV, 4,889 investors, 0% default. But investor anxiety around production-linked repayment delays was growing. The strategic response: launch a Category II AIF with a structured lock-in period that aligned investor expectations with the natural rhythm of entertainment finance. Stop fighting the timeline problem — design a product built around it. I owned the marketing and communications end to end.

The Problem

  • A new, regulated financial product going to market in a category most Indian investors had never encountered
  • Had to convert existing investors and attract new HNI and institutional capital simultaneously
  • Investor sentiment still recovering — the AIF launch had to rebuild confidence, not just announce a product
  • Marketing had to feel sophisticated and trustworthy for a SEBI-registered fund — not promotional
  • Founding team needed a credible external voice to support high-value fundraising conversations

What I Did

Built the AIF Investor Narrative

Developed the core positioning: entertainment finance is recession-resilient, culturally relevant, backed by pre-sold rights. The lock-in period wasn't a constraint — it was the solution. A structured fund with a defined horizon aligns capital with the natural lifecycle of content rights monetisation.

Wrote the Investor Pitch Deck

Structured the full pitch — ₹462+ Cr GTV, 0% default, 49 producers, buyers including Netflix, Prime Video, Disney+ Hotstar; India M&E market (₹2.3T → ₹3.1T by 2026 per EY); fund terms (₹300 Cr, 16–18% IRR, 4+1+1 years); team credentials. Complex mechanics made intelligible without oversimplifying.

Designed Launch Campaigns + Founder Personal Branding

Built content strategy, messaging architecture, and channel execution for HNI and institutional segments. Simultaneously developed the founders' external narrative — CEO Pradeep Somu and Co-Founder Sethu Rajendran. In regulated, high-trust categories, founder credibility is a material marketing asset.

Christmas Investor Engagement Campaign

Designed a seasonal branded comic during the pre-launch period — to humanise the brand and give investors a reason to stay connected outside transaction moments. In trust-sensitive categories, the brands that show up creatively between deal cycles are the ones investors return to.
